"Willpower & Flow"

Interactive Art



My artwork is willpower and flow. Willpower represents relying on my inner strength to push through the struggles of self-defeating, mental fatigue, and procrastination. Flow is finding my balance of enjoying what I create and finding a common ground that leads the enjoyment into excitement rather than using willpower or force. 2020 has put me and other artists in a self-defeating mental state. As this pandemic continues, being on Zoom calls and doing class homework 8 hours are more does more harm than productivity.

Start in Project 1, I explore A-frame to make an experience of a virtual roller coaster mixed in with the pandemic toilet paper madness. I incorporate and explore scales, motion, sound, and interactive movement through Web Vr. Motion is used in the three-ring to create an illusion of a blur and busy reality with things coming at you. The head is used as a representation of being stuck in daily tasks. The eerie stimulating sound help speeds up anxiety and stress level which causes more overall fatigue. Last but not least, noticed there is a sand dial as a representation of time. Feeling of isolation, anxiety, procrastination, and being on a grind due to the workload.

For Project 2, I utilize A-frame, Sketchfab, and/or three js to help with this work. Diving deeper into A-frame this time, I created a live web browser garden for everyone to access and be aware of the dichotomy of online learning. I speak on the technology being a double edge sword where one can gain comfort working from home but that costs us human social interaction and gain no further guidance from our teachers and friends since we are all alone. Unlike the first project for an immersive VR space, this project is more so of an online wall installation due to the physical limitation of the viewer. This gallery wall is right in front and the message is in the center. I took this opportunity to create something more simple for accessibility and having traditional (painting) art mixed with a live virtual event (wallflower) art.

Last, in Project 3, I look to Carsten Holler and Bill Viola for my inspiration. Most of Carsten's work involves interactive spatial art such as giant mushroom upside-down sculptures where you can navigate around in the physical space. In that piece, he used movement, visual, and spatial to force the audience to interact with his artwork. The second person was Bill Viola, who focuses on human consciousness and uses digital video to express his work. Both embodied the concept of interaction based storytelling artwork through experience and sensations. This gave me an idea to explore the idea of creating an online theme park since a physical one is not possible. I started off again with the basics of A-Frame building my foundation up to explore deeper concept and scalability. I've found common threads between Bill's and Carsten's work to draw inspiration from. First, it has to be interaction, and second, it has to be video. I took this opportunity to explore the concept of python turtle generative art. I found tutorials online that would teach me how to set up the environment and figure out the coding part. Python turtle is a library of python to make visual shapes and graphs. I spend the time to record my process and edit the video of those outputted codes. I would use those videos and visuals for my A-Frame theme park on the web as a feature piece to the online park. The end result was an engaging visual output of multiple shapes with tacky colors and a video recorded from the work. Last but not least, I got to learned A-Frame and Python to make an online park that incorporates video and interaction-based artwork for my project 3 related to willpower and flow.
